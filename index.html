Jacksonville Linux Users' Group™ (JaxLUG™)

Jacksonville Linux Users' Group™, JaxLUG™, is a community users group comprised of a diverse group of local Linux users in the greater Jacksonville, Florida metropolitan area, ranging from beginners, home users, systems administrators, developers, and IT professionals to general Linux and Free and Open Source Software (FOSS) technology enthusiasts. The JaxLUG exists to add value to members by attracting leading technology professionals to present on innovative Linux development methodologies and technologies. North Florida Linux users of any kind are welcome!

The JaxLUG, originally started by Daniel Stringfield in 1996 and is presently rebooting in 2025, yes, every now and then even Linux must reboot. You are welcome to be part of what is next for the JaxLUG, we are seeking to fulfill new roles on the JaxLUG board and officer positions. If you would like an official legal role and title with the Jacksonville Linux Users Group, the JaxLUG, then get in touch with us!

We are presently conducting a public review of draft bylaws to be adopted and filed with the State of Florida once approved by the membership; no set cut off date. If you have feedback, ideas, suggestions or critique, please open an issue using the subject as the topic and voice your concerns. This is your chance to shape the future direction of the JaxLUG!

To get to know the group become a member of the JaxLUG, join our Discord Server, or participate in our Monthly Meetings!

Discord

We have a JaxLUG Discord server available for a variety of uses. If you are new to Linux, come and join us, we seek to answer all your basic questions, and of course, anyone not new to Linux is welcome in the JaxLUG Discord server as well. In the JaxLUG discord server you can meet people with various interests and backgrounds, who are all passionate about Linux in some form or another. Come learn or geek out about a Linux topic of interest and join a discussions in our brand new JaxLUG Discord Server.

JaxLUG announcements and event updates are available first thru via the JaxLUG Discord server. Make sure to stay up to date with the group events!

Donations

Now that Jacksonville Linux Users Group is a Florida Non-Profit, 501(c)(3) under IRC, which will take 5 years to receive permanent status from the IRS, but, in the mean time, anyone can now do tax deductible donations to the JaxLUG! We are working on setting up ways for you to donate. For the immediate time being the money will be used for administrative purposes, legal filings, trademark, domain, etc. In the future, as the JaxLUG's resources grow these funds will be put to use by the JaxLUG board to benefit the Jacksonville Florida Metropolitan area to foster the education and usage of Linux!

Presently, we are looking into donation platforms and will provide links ASAP, stay tuned!

Be the Presenter

We welcome and encourage anyone, be it local, outsider, hobbyist, professional, student, or other Linux enthusiast, to be a presenter at any of our monthly meetings, with a topic of their choosing, or one the group has requested. Simply provide a Title and a Synopsis of the presentation to a board member or officer of the JaxLUG, at this time, we will be working on other ways to formalize the presentation application process. Please, provide at least one month notice for us to confirm and add the presentation Title and Synopsis to our calendar.

For students, this is a great way to meet state requirements on public presentations and looks better on your resume than in-class presentations. Students are HIGHLY encouraged to present!

Meeting Format

  • 6:00 PM - Regular arrival, Setup
  • 6:15 PM - JaxLUG Old/New biz, Other local events, Introductions.
  • 6:30 PM - Presentation, Topics, Q&A time.
  • 8:30 PM - Conclusion, Summary, and clean-up.
  • 8:45 PM - Linux Users' Social Hour (LUSH)
           Join us afterwards for more conversations and discussions. Presenter's place of choice.

JaxLUG History

The JaxLUG has a long history, and history must be learned from or it will be repeated. While the JaxLUG has survived for going on four decades, it is not without controversy and issues that are the motivation and reasoning behind the recent legal changes and forthcoming structural changes. To learn about how the JaxLUG got here, we are creating some pages to cover some of the pivotal moments in the JaxLUG history.

We aim to cover the good not just the controversial, and please feel free to open issues with any suggestions and/or corrections.
